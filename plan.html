Revenue streams

Four ways to earn.
Each feeds the others.

The streams are deliberately complementary — school courses build credibility for employers, 1:1s convert from people who attended courses, evening courses build the public brand.

Stream 1
Evening courses — public
£75 per person · max 12
  • Target: 1-2 courses per month from September onwards
  • Venue: community centres, co-working spaces across Bristol
  • Channel: Meta ads (teacher targeting), Google search, local Facebook groups, flyers
  • Full course £900 revenue · realistic avg group size 8-10 = £600-750
  • Costs: ~£45/course (venue + materials)
Stream 2
School / teacher courses
£99 per head · 12-30 people
  • Target: 1 school cohort per month from September onwards
  • Includes TPS bonus session — unique differentiator
  • Channel: LinkedIn (heads/DHTs), direct school outreach, your headteacher network
  • 12 people = £1,188 · 20 people = £1,980 · near-zero delivery cost
  • Your biggest margin stream — prioritise this
Stream 3
Employer / organisation courses
£800–£1,200 per cohort
  • Target: 1 employer course per quarter — don't over-rely on this
  • Pipeline: construction workers (bro-in-law), Longleat (father-in-law), others
  • Channel: LinkedIn outreach to HR/wellbeing leads, employers page, referrals
  • Close the warm leads first — these exist now and need a direct ask
  • Each one validates the next — build case studies from every delivery
Stream 4
1:1 retirement forecasting
£300 per session · 1-2 hours
  • Target: 1-2 sessions per month by autumn, 3 by spring
  • Source: course attendees who want personalised follow-up, direct enquiries
  • Zero delivery cost — highest margin activity you have
  • Especially valuable for teachers wanting TPS + personal pension modelling
  • Keep strictly educational — cashflow modelling, not regulated advice

Marketing actions

What to actually do
in each channel.

Not vague strategy — specific, actionable things ordered by expected impact for your situation.

🎓
Teacher targeting — highest priority
  • Facebook ads: target teachers in Bristol (job title targeting + teaching groups). Use "By a teacher, for teachers" angle. Budget £3-5/day.
  • Google Search: keywords "investing course for teachers bristol", "teachers pension scheme explained bristol" — low competition, high intent.
  • LinkedIn: connect with heads, DHTs and school business managers. Post 2x/week about financial literacy in schools. Share pilot results.
  • Your head's LinkedIn schmooze — hold her to this. Send her the promo video link and a suggested caption she can post.
  • Teacher Facebook groups: Bristol Teachers, NQT groups. Short personal post, not an ad.
🏢
Employer outreach — close warm leads first
  • Construction workers: ask your brother-in-law to set up a 10-minute call with whoever makes the decision. Give him a one-pager PDF to forward this week.
  • Longleat: same approach via your father-in-law. "Just need 10 minutes with your HR/wellbeing person."
  • LinkedIn outreach: search "HR Manager Bristol" or "People Director Bristol" — personalised connection note referencing financial wellbeing stats.
  • Once first employer case study exists, use it in all future outreach — "We ran this at [Company] and here's what happened."
🌍
Public evening courses — organic first
  • Facebook community groups: shorter posts, poll-style hook. "Did your school ever teach you anything useful about money?" Drop link in comments.
  • Eventbrite: keep listing sharp with testimonials and updated description. It has its own discovery — don't neglect it.
  • Flyers: hyper-local, Brooklands Park estate and nearby streets. £15-20 for 250 flyers. "Local maths teacher" angle is powerful here.
  • Retargeting ads: only run to people who've visited your site. Much cheaper, much warmer. Even £2/day.
  • Email list: message every subscriber personally when a new date opens. Not a newsletter blast — a personal email.
👤
1:1 retirement forecasting — lowest effort, highest margin
  • Email every past attendee with a personal note: "I've started offering 1:1 retirement planning sessions — £300 for a thorough session. Particularly useful for teachers thinking about TPS alongside their own investing."
  • Add it prominently to your website — it's currently not clearly advertised.
  • Mention it at the end of every course: "If you want to go deeper on your specific situation, I offer private sessions."
  • Especially valuable for attendees in their 40s/50s thinking about retirement — your pilot had several of these.
📰
Press and credibility
  • Bristol 24/7 and Bristol Live pitches — send from your wife's email this week if not already sent.
  • Pitch and Shout: respond to every relevant callout within 24 hours. Financial resilience, education, wellbeing angles all relevant.
  • One press feature does more for trust than months of ads — prioritise this.
  • Google Reviews: personally ask every single attendee. 10 reviews transforms how cold visitors perceive you.
  • Blog: two articles already — keep adding one per month. "How the Teachers' Pension Scheme works" will rank for long-tail search.
🔄
Referrals and word of mouth
  • Every attendee is a potential referrer. Send a personal WhatsApp 2 weeks after the course: "Hope the investing is going well — if you know anyone who'd benefit, I'd love to have them along."
  • Bring-a-friend: £68 each when booking together. Make sure this is clearly stated near the booking section.
  • Ivan's video testimonial — make sure it's front and centre on your landing page and in your ads. A real face converts far better than text.
  • Ask your headteacher to share your promo video on her LinkedIn to other heads — give her the exact text to post so she doesn't have to think about it.
