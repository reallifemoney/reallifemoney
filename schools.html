Why financial wellbeing matters for school staff

Financial stress can directly impact staff retention and overall wellbeing. Providing clear educational training helps staff make informed decisions about their earnings and futures.

The need for literacy

A national poll of over 4,000 secondary school teachers showed that 78 per cent of schools rely on personal, social, health and economic teachers to deliver financial literacy to students[cite: 144]. However, only 27 per cent of maths teachers do so[cite: 144]. Crucially, non-specialist teachers are far less likely to feel comfortable navigating or explaining complex lessons on investing, mortgages, or pensions themselves[cite: 144].

Pension complexity

The Teachers' Pension Scheme is highly comprehensive but far from straightforward[cite: 142]. Staff frequently express confusion over how their career average contributions stack up, what to expect at retirement age, or how additional contribution adjustments work[cite: 142, 143].

Concrete school benefits

Providing a structured, low-cost financial personal development module is a measurable benefit that demonstrates clear investment in staff care, leading to higher baseline engagement across teams[cite: 141].

Available course modules

Based on pilot group analysis from our sessions at Castle School, we run practical, jargon-free workshops that feature gamified physical assets to keep staff actively focused[cite: 129, 135].

Core course track

The beginner's guide to investing [cite: 96]

Format: 2 × 90-minute practical modules, strictly limited to a maximum of 12 attendees per group to support personalized questions[cite: 156].

  • Session 1: Why invest, breaking down asset types, and managing individual risk tracks[cite: 157].
  • Session 2: Selecting accounts (ISAs/options), picking diversified funds, and safely executing transactions[cite: 158].
Integrated inclusion

Teachers' Pension Scheme demystified [cite: 159, 161]

Format: Bonus 60-minute session open to up to 30 staff members simultaneously (not restricted to core course participants)[cite: 160, 162].

  • Breaks down how career average calculations accrue over service[cite: 163].
  • Maps real retirement timelines, early retirement reduction scales, and top-up choices[cite: 163].
  • Delivered using practical scenario models rather than passive, text-heavy lectures[cite: 164].

Flexible school deployment models

Model A: Fully funded

£100 / attendee

The school pays per core attendee. This includes the full investing pathway for up to 12 staff, plus the bonus pension session is unlocked completely free of charge for up to 30 staff members[cite: 171].

Model B: Pension only

£20 / attendee

The school provides funding for the standalone Teachers' Pension Scheme masterclass group block. Staff can choose to buy into the separate investment tracks privately if desired[cite: 174, 175].

Model C: Subsidised

Co-funded

The school covers a set percentage portion of the per-head course fee, and staff members pay the remainder, keeping investment outlays low while maintaining skin in the game[cite: 178, 179].

Model D: Room provided

£0 to the school

The school provides a physical room location and handles internal email promotion to staff. The cost passes entirely to individual staff members at a special private rate of £85 per head[cite: 181, 182, 183, 184].
